It's easy to describe the relative position of two single points, but how can we measure the spatial relationship between more complicated objects? Here, we consider simple 2D boxes and some ways to capture their relative position.

This webpage is an interactive supplement for our more in-depth articles that describe how to compute these measures in more detail.

Consider a pair of 2D boxes as shown in the figure below. How would you describe the relative position of the dark red box with respect to the dark blue box? In particular, is their spatial configuration similar to that of the light blue and red boxes? You can drag the boxes around and resize them to see how different similarity measures evaluate this question. We consider two approaches, one using triangular fuzzy numbers (TFN) and another using histograms of forces (HoF). Note that in this example for the TFN methods, the boxes are weighted with a center of mass that can be adjusted.

The TFN methods are simple to compute, however the HoF approach is more computationally demanding. Use the buttons above to adjust the quality of the HoF calculation or to turn it off completely to improve performance.
